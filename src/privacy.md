Privacy Policy: Douban Mastodon Sync
Last Updated: April 20, 2026

1. Introduction
This Privacy Policy describes how "Douban Mastodon Sync" handles user data. Our primary goal is to provide a seamless synchronization service between Douban and Mastodon while respecting your privacy.

2. Data Collection and Usage
As disclosed in our Chrome Web Store Privacy disclosures, we handle the following types of data:

Authentication Information: We collect and store Mastodon OAuth access tokens and instance URLs. This data is stored locally on your device using chrome.storage and is used solely to authenticate your posts to your Mastodon account.

Website Content: When you trigger a sync, the extension reads the text and media content of your Douban posts. This data is processed locally to format it for the Mastodon API.

3. Data Storage and Security
Local Storage: All configuration data and authentication tokens are stored locally on your machine.

No Remote Servers: This extension does not transmit your data to any third-party servers other than the official Mastodon API of the instance you have configured. We do not operate a middle-man server.

Encryption: We use modern cryptography for any data transmitted to the Mastodon API (HTTPS).

4. Data Sharing and Disclosure
No Sale of Data: We do not sell, trade, or rent user data to third parties.

Limited Use: Data is only used to fulfill the "Single Purpose" of syncing posts from Douban to Mastodon. We do not use your data for advertising, marketing, or creditworthiness.

5. Your Rights
Since all data is stored locally, you can delete all information collected by the extension at any time by:

Clearing the extension's storage in the settings.

Uninstalling the extension from Chrome.
